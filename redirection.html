Indus Valley Civilization

One of the world's earliest urban civilizations, the Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE) was known for its planned cities, advanced drainage systems, standardized weights, and impressive architecture.

Major Cities: Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Dholavira
Known For: Town planning, Great Bath, script, seals

Maurya & Gupta Empires

The Maurya Empire (322–185 BCE), founded by Chandragupta Maurya, became one of the largest empires in Indian history. Under Ashoka, it achieved great cultural and political influence.

Ashoka: Spread Buddhism, built stupas, erected Edict Pillars.

The Gupta Empire (320–550 CE) is known as the “Golden Age of India.” It produced major achievements in science, mathematics, astronomy, literature, and art.

Achievements: Aryabhata’s astronomy, zero in mathematics, classical Sanskrit literature.

Culture, Science & Knowledge

Ancient India contributed significantly to world knowledge. Texts like the Vedas, Upanishads, Mahabharata, and Ramayana shaped philosophy and culture for centuries.

Key Contributions:
  • Yoga & Meditation
  • Ayurveda – ancient medical science
  • Mathematics – Zero, decimal system
  • Architecture – Temples, stupas
  • Literature – Sanskrit epics
